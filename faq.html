Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about FoodRescue — food safety, eligibility, process, and privacy.

🍽️ For Food Donors
You can donate any fresh, cooked food that meets FSSAI guidelines: freshly cooked meals (within 4 hours), properly refrigerated food (within 24 hours), fresh bakery items (same-day), and packaged foods with valid expiry dates. You cannot donate: food that was left out >4 hours at room temperature, spoiled or expired food, alcohol, or items with known allergens not disclosed.
Yes. All food donors on FoodRescue must hold a valid FSSAI license. This is to protect both recipients and donors legally. We verify your 14-digit FSSAI number during registration. If your license is under renewal, contact our team to arrange interim verification.
FoodRescue operates under the Food Safety and Standards (Food Donation) Regulations, 2019, which provide Good Samaritan protections to verified donors who act in good faith. Each donation is timestamped, photo-verified, and safety-scored. Our platform creates a full audit trail that protects you legally. Always ensure food meets our safety standards before posting.
If no NGO within the initial 5 km radius accepts within the urgency window, our system automatically expands to 10 km, then 15 km. If still unaccepted, you'll receive a notification and the donation will be marked as expired. Our team is working on expanding NGO partnerships to reduce this rate to <5% of all donations.
After each completed donation, an automated PDF certificate is generated and emailed to your registered address. Annual CSR certificates (aggregating all 12-month donations) are generated in January and include SDG 2, 12, 13, 17 compliance reporting. Download anytime from Impact → Dashboard.
🏢 For NGOs
Required: NGO registration certificate (Societies Act or Trust Act), PAN number, address proof, contact details of coordinator. Optional but recommended: 12A/80G certificate, FCRA registration (for international donors), capacity details (meals/day). Verification takes 2–5 business days.
NGO reliability scores are tracked. Consistent rejection without reasons (e.g., capacity full) lowers your reliability score, which affects your position in the matching algorithm. We understand that NGOs have real capacity constraints — please update your daily capacity in the dashboard so the system matches you appropriately.
🛵 For Volunteers
All volunteers must complete a 1-hour food safety module covering: temperature danger zones, proper handling, signs of spoilage, QR handoff process, and emergency protocol. A quiz with minimum 70% score is required before your first delivery. The module is available in English, Hindi, and Gujarati.
Every successful delivery earns 50–100 points depending on distance and urgency. Bonus points for emergency deliveries (+50), perfect-rating deliveries (+25), and milestones. Tiers: 🌱 Seedling (0–499), 🪴 Sapling (500–1999), 🛡️ Guardian (2000+). Points can be redeemed for platform badges, community recognition, and partner discounts.
🔒 Privacy & Safety
All data is stored on Firebase with end-to-end security rules. Phone numbers and addresses are only shared with matched parties during an active donation. GPS location is only tracked during active deliveries and is deleted after completion. We comply with India's Personal Data Protection framework. See our full Privacy Policy for details.
Government ID photos are stored in encrypted Firebase Storage with strict access controls — only admin verification staff can view them. After verification, the raw images are archived and deleted from active storage within 90 days. We never share ID documents with donors, NGOs, or other volunteers.
