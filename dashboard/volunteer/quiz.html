Module 1: Temperature Safety Step 1 of 4

🌡️ FSSAI Temperature Danger Zones

To prevent dangerous bacterial growth, food must be kept outside the "Danger Zone" (5°C to 60°C). Hot cooked food must be dispatched, delivered, and consumed within 4 hours of preparation.

Critical Rule: If food sits at room temperature for over 4 hours, it must not be delivered. Cancel dispatch and flag immediately.

📦 Safe Handoff Handovers

Always ensure food containers are securely sealed, leak-proof, and kept upright during transport. Do not open food packages or expose them to dust/dirt.

Cleanliness standard: Sanitize hands before pickup. Ensure storage boxes inside delivery vehicles are clean.

🚨 Spotting and Rejecting Spoiled Items

Inspect the item details on pickup. Signs of spoilage include sour odors, fermentation gas (swollen packaging), mold growth, or discoloration. Never proceed with delivery if these signs are detected.
