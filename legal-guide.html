Legal Protections

Donor Liability Protections

How India's Food Safety and Standards (Food Donation) Regulations, 2019 protect establishments that donate food in good faith.

Indian Good Samaritan Legislation

Historically, high-end hotels and restaurants in India discarded edible surplus food because of liability concerns: what if a recipient fell ill? To address this, the Food Safety and Standards Authority of India (FSSAI) gazetted the **Food Safety and Standards (Food Donation) Regulations in 2019**.

Key Protection Principle: Good Faith

Donors who handle surplus food in compliance with basic hygiene requirements (clean storage, temperature logs, non-spoilage inspection) before handoff are legally protected from civil or criminal liability.

01

FSSAI Compliance Duty

Establishments must maintain FSSAI permit registration files, verify safety logs (e.g. food preparation times), and pack donations in food-grade packaging containers.

02

NGO Recipient Trust

Registered NGOs are responsible for maintaining safe transit temperatures and ensuring clean distribution channels. This clearly shifts responsibility after a secure QR handoff.
